# Day 4: Reaction Engineering & Catalysis

## Learning Objectives
- Understand reactor design principles and types
- Master reaction kinetics and catalyst fundamentals
- Connect reactor performance to profitability
- Prepare examples of reactor optimization and innovation

## Key Topics to Review

### Reaction Kinetics
- Rate laws and reaction orders
- Temperature effects (Arrhenius equation)
- Catalyst kinetics and mechanisms
- Complex reactions (series, parallel, recycle)

### Reactor Types
- Batch reactors and their applications
- Continuous stirred-tank reactors (CSTR)
- Plug flow reactors (PFR)
- Fixed bed vs. fluidized bed reactors

### Catalysis
- Homogeneous vs. heterogeneous catalysis
- Catalyst deactivation and regeneration
- Selectivity and yield optimization
- Catalyst characterization techniques

### Business Connection Points
- Conversion and selectivity impact on profitability
- Catalyst life cycle costs
- Reactor safety considerations
- Innovation in reactor technology

## Daily Tasks
1. Review reactor design equations (design equation applications)
2. Practice calculating conversion and selectivity
3. Study industrial catalyst examples and their applications
4. Prepare examples of reactor/process innovations

## Interview Preparation Questions
- How do you choose the right reactor type for a process?
- What factors affect catalyst performance over time?
- How would you improve the efficiency of a reactor system?
- Describe the role of catalysis in sustainable chemistry

## Resources for Review
- Elements of Chemical Reaction Engineering by Fogler
- Chemical Reaction Engineering by Levenspiel
- Catalysis in Industry case studies
