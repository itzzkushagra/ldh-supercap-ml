# Day 7: Integration & Mock Interview Preparation

## Learning Objectives
- Integrate all major chemical engineering concepts learned during the week
- Prepare for technical questions in management interviews
- Practice explaining complex concepts simply
- Develop compelling examples linking engineering to business value

## Key Activities

### Knowledge Integration
- Connect process engineering concepts across all domains
- Understand how unit operations work together in complete processes
- Review the economic and business implications of technical decisions
- Practice translating technical achievements into business value

### Interview Preparation
- Common technical questions in management interviews
- STAR method for answering behavioral questions
- Explaining technical concepts to non-technical audiences
- Connecting past projects to leadership and strategic thinking

### Case Study Preparation
- Prepare 2-3 detailed examples of technical projects
- Quantify impact of process improvements
- Identify lessons learned and leadership moments
- Practice converting technical metrics to business metrics

### Mock Interview Practice
- Technical competency questions
- Leadership and project management scenarios
- Industry trend awareness
- Communication of complex ideas simply

## Daily Tasks
1. Create a comprehensive mind map connecting all topics covered
2. Prepare 3 technical examples with clear business outcomes
3. Practice explaining complex concepts in simple terms
4. Conduct mock interviews focusing on technical-to-management transition

## Potential Interview Questions
- How does your engineering background prepare you for management?
- Describe a challenging technical problem you solved
- How would you approach improving operational efficiency?
- What trends do you see in the chemical industry?
- Tell me about a time you led a technical project
- How do you handle conflicts between technical requirements and business needs?

## Success Metrics
By the end of Day 7, you should be able to:
- Confidently discuss technical concepts with non-technical interviewers
- Articulate the business value of engineering decisions
- Provide specific examples of technical leadership
- Demonstrate understanding of industry challenges and opportunities
- Connect your technical background to your management aspirations
