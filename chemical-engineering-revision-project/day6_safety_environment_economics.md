# Day 6: Safety, Environment & Economics

## Learning Objectives
- Understand process safety fundamentals and risk assessment
- Review environmental regulations and sustainability concepts
- Master chemical engineering economics and project evaluation
- Connect safety/environmental compliance to business value

## Key Topics to Review

### Process Safety
- Hazard identification (HAZOP, What-if analysis)
- Risk assessment and management
- Safety instrumented systems (SIL ratings)
- Emergency response planning
- Inherently safer design principles

### Environmental Engineering
- Emission control technologies
- Waste minimization strategies
- Water treatment and recycling
- Carbon footprint and climate change
- Circular economy principles

### Engineering Economics
- Present worth and annual cost analysis
- Rate of return and payback period
- Depreciation and tax considerations
- Sensitivity analysis
- Uncertainty and risk analysis

### Business Connection Points
- Compliance costs vs. risk mitigation
- Sustainability as competitive advantage
- ROI of safety investments
- ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) considerations

## Daily Tasks
1. Review safety assessment methodologies
2. Study environmental regulations affecting chemical industry
3. Practice economic evaluation calculations
4. Research sustainable process examples

## Interview Preparation Questions
- How do you balance safety investments with profitability?
- What role does sustainability play in modern chemical plants?
- How would you evaluate a new project considering both economics and safety?
- Describe how process safety connects to business continuity
