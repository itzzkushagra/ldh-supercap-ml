# Quick Reference Guide: Chemical Engineering for IIM Interviews

## Core Concepts Summary

### Process Engineering
- **Material Balance**: Input = Output + Accumulation
- **Energy Balance**: Based on first law of thermodynamics
- **Key Equipment**: Pumps, compressors, heat exchangers, distillation columns, reactors
- **Business Value**: Process efficiency, cost reduction, product quality

### Thermodynamics
- **First Law**: Energy conservation (Î”U = Q - W)
- **Second Law**: Entropy increases, efficiency limits
- **Applications**: Energy recovery, phase equilibrium, process feasibility
- **Business Value**: Energy efficiency, utility optimization

### Mass Transfer
- **Key Processes**: Distillation, absorption, extraction, adsorption
- **Design Factors**: Theoretical stages, reflux ratios, pressure drops
- **Business Value**: Product purity, separation efficiency, energy consumption

### Reaction Engineering
- **Rate Laws**: r = kC^n (temperature dependent via Arrhenius)
- **Reactor Types**: Batch, CSTR, PFR with different characteristics
- **Catalysts**: Increase rate, improve selectivity, reduce energy
- **Business Value**: Conversion efficiency, yield optimization

### Process Control
- **PID Controllers**: Proportional-Integral-Derivative for stability
- **Advanced Control**: MPC for multivariable optimization
- **Business Value**: Consistency, quality, safety, efficiency

### Safety & Economics
- **Risk Assessment**: HAZOP, LOPA, SIL ratings
- **Economic Measures**: NPV, IRR, payback period
- **Business Value**: Risk mitigation, compliance, profitability

## Common Interview Questions & Answers

### "Explain your engineering background."
*Sample Response*: "As a chemical engineer, I've developed expertise in process design and optimization. My background involves designing and improving manufacturing processes, ensuring safety and efficiency while reducing costs. This experience taught me to approach complex problems systematically and consider multiple variables simultaneously - skills valuable in management."

### "How does your technical background apply to business?"
*Sample Response*: "Engineering taught me to optimize systems for efficiency and cost-effectiveness. Every decision involves trade-offs between capital investment, operating costs, safety, and quality - similar to business strategy. I learned to quantify improvements and justify investments based on data."

### "What's an example of a problem you solved?"
*Sample Response*: "At [previous company], we had low yields in our [process]. I analyzed the system, identified bottlenecks, implemented changes to temperature controls and feed ratios, resulting in 15% yield improvement and $X in annual savings. This required cross-functional collaboration and systematic troubleshooting."
